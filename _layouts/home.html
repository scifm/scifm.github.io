AI for Scientific Discovery

AI is reshaping how we ask and answer scientific questions—turning data, simulation, and theory into a continuous engine for discovery. Scientific Foundation Models (SciFMs) promise to learn across modalities and domains, to accelerate experiments, and to help scientists reason at scale. Development of scientific foundation model

SciFMs are large, pre-trained scientific AI models that learn from multimodal scientific data — text, images, spectra, graphs, and simulations. By embedding physical laws and domain knowledge, they provide a reusable foundation across materials, chemistry, and physics to speed discovery, tighten the loop between theory and experiment, and enable automated scientific reasoning.

Vision

We envision SciFMs as collaborative scientific partners: models that suggest hypotheses, design experiments, and synthesize knowledge across disciplines. They should reduce repetition, reveal hidden patterns, and empower researchers to explore bolder ideas faster.

Goals

  • Build robust multimodal models grounded in physics and first principles.
  • Integrate models into experiment and simulation workflows for rapid iteration.
  • Foster open, reproducible tools and datasets for the community.
